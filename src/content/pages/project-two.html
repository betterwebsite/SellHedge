Approaches for Valuing Your Company

The three generally accepted approaches to valuing a company.

  • Asset Approach

    The asset approach values a business by adding up its assets and subtracting its liabilities. However, this figure may not fully reflect the true worth of a profitable, ongoing company.

    01

    Asset Approach
  • Market Approach

    The market approach works much like real estate comps, comparing recent sales of similar businesses. By analyzing industry databases for multiples, revenue, and earnings, this method is often the most reliable way to estimate value.

    02

    Market Approach
  • Income Approach

    Most valuation methods factor in projected growth to determine value. The income approach does this by measuring what your business is worth today based on the future income it’s expected to generate for an investor.

    03

    Income Approach
